# Blockchain-Based-KYC-Model-for-Credit-Allocation-in-Banking
Blockchain-Based KYC Model for Credit Allocation in Banking is a secure and decentralized banking solution that leverages blockchain technology to simplify the Know Your Customer (KYC) verification process and improve the efficiency of credit allocation. Traditional KYC systems require customers to repeatedly submit the same documents to different financial institutions, resulting in higher costs, delays, and increased risk of data breaches.

This project uses a blockchain network to store customer KYC records in an immutable and tamper-proof manner. Once a customer's identity is verified by an authorized bank, the verified KYC information is securely recorded on the blockchain. Other participating banks can access the verified records with the customer's consent, eliminating duplicate verification and significantly reducing processing time.

The system also integrates a credit allocation module that evaluates customer eligibility based on verified KYC information, credit score, income details, employment status, repayment history, and other financial parameters. Smart contracts automate the verification and loan approval process by enforcing predefined lending rules, ensuring transparency, accuracy, and reducing manual intervention.

To enhance security, sensitive customer documents are encrypted and stored off-chain, while only their cryptographic hash values and metadata are stored on the blockchain to maintain data integrity and privacy. Role-based authentication ensures that only authorized users can access specific information.

The project provides separate interfaces for customers, bank officials, and administrators. Customers can register, upload documents, grant or revoke access to their KYC data, and track loan application status. Bank officials can verify customer information, assess creditworthiness, and approve or reject loan requests. Administrators manage participating banks, monitor transactions, and maintain the blockchain network.
